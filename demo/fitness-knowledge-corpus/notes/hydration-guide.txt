Hydration guide for active people:

Water intake affects training quality, recovery and day-to-day wellbeing. A
simple rule is to drink regularly across the day rather than trying to correct
everything right before a workout.

During hot weather or high sweat sessions, hydration needs increase. People who
train intensely should also pay attention to sodium and general electrolyte
balance, especially if workouts are long.

Dark urine, headaches, unusual fatigue and reduced exercise performance can be
signs that hydration is too low.

Hydration is a support habit. It does not replace sleep, calories or protein,
but it improves the likelihood that the overall training plan works as intended.
